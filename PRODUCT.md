# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Compradora (buyer):** Anyone who arrives looking for something specific — a gift, a service, a trusted local business. She may have heard about the directory through the SW Mujeres Facebook community, through word of mouth, or through search. She doesn't need to be a member of anything. She's on her phone, she's busy, she has zero patience for friction. She wants to find the right person fast, confirm she's trustworthy, and contact her directly.

**Empresaria (entrepreneur):** A verified member of SW Mujeres who wants her business visible to 13,500 potential buyers. She's proud of her work. She submitted a form and waited for approval. The profile page is her storefront — it must make her look professional and trustworthy.

**Administradora (admin):** Reviews applications, manages memberships, monitors finances. Works in the admin panel — a tool surface, not a brand surface. Needs clarity and efficiency, not visual drama.

## Product Purpose

DirectorioSW solves ad fatigue: instead of interrupting women with commercial posts (46%+ rejection rate in the Facebook group), the directory lets buyers arrive *looking* for products. It's a curated, verified directory of women entrepreneurs in Medellín — not a marketplace, not an app store. A warm, trustworthy place to find someone you can rely on.

Success looks like: a compradora finds the right business in under 30 seconds, confirms she's verified SW, and taps WhatsApp. No account. No friction. One tap.

## Brand Personality

**Curaduría, confianza, ahorro de tiempo.**

Voice: direct, warm, confident. Not corporate. Not startup-speak. The tone of a trusted friend saying "te recomiendo a esta señora, es increíble."

Emotional goal: the compradora feels she's in a curated space, not a dump of listings. The empresaria feels her business is presented with dignity.

## Anti-references

- Generic e-commerce marketplaces (Mercado Libre, Amazon) — impersonal, transactional, visual noise
- Infinite grids of identical cards — no hierarchy, no curation signal
- Excel-style filter panels — technical, cold, wrong register
- Text-heavy layouts — no one reads it; trust is built through visual cues, not paragraphs
- Corporate directory aesthetics — this is a community of real women, not a B2B vendor list

## Design Principles

1. **Curated, not listed.** Every layout choice should signal that someone made deliberate decisions about what goes here. Hierarchy over abundance. Restraint over completeness.

2. **Trust through texture.** The SW badge, the warm palette, the verified status — these are the signals that make a compradora reach for her phone. Surface them clearly. Never bury them.

3. **Time is the product.** Every extra tap, every wall of text, every filter that requires thought is a failure. The fastest path to "contact this person" is the right design.

4. **Community, not marketplace.** The visual language should feel like a warm recommendation from someone in the group, not a product listing in a store. Human-first, not catalog-first.

5. **Dignity for the empresaria.** Each profile is a woman's livelihood. The design must make her look as professional as she is — not clip-art amateur, not impersonal SaaS.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Spanish-language audience, Medellín, Colombia. All copy in Spanish.
- Primary device: mobile phone (WhatsApp-native users). Mobile-first is non-negotiable.
- WCAG AA minimum for contrast. The burgundy palette has inherent contrast challenges at lighter values — verify all text combinations.
- No specific reduced-motion requirements stated, but animations should be subtle (community tool, not a tech product).
